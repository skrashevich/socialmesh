# Mesh vs Internet

Meshtastic mesh networks and the internet both let you communicate, but they work in fundamentally different ways. Understanding these differences helps you know when each is the right tool.

## How They Differ

|                    | Internet                                      | Mesh Network                                 |
| ------------------ | --------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| **Infrastructure** | Requires cell towers, fibre, routers, servers | Zero infrastructure — just radios            |
| **Cost**           | Subscriptions, data plans                     | Free after hardware purchase                 |
| **Range**          | Global                                        | Local to regional (depends on mesh coverage) |
| **Speed**          | Fast (Mbps to Gbps)                           | Slow (a few kbps)                            |
| **Reliability**    | Depends on infrastructure uptime              | Works as long as nodes have power            |
| **Privacy**        | Traffic passes through ISPs and servers       | Peer-to-peer, encrypted on the mesh          |
| **Content**        | Any digital content                           | Short text, GPS, small telemetry             |

## When Mesh Wins

The mesh shines in situations where internet infrastructure is unavailable, unreliable, or undesirable:

- **Remote areas** — mountains, forests, ocean, desert
- **Natural disasters** — when cell towers lose power or are damaged
- **Events with overloaded networks** — festivals, large gatherings
- **Privacy-sensitive communication** — no third-party servers involved
- **Off-grid living** — no monthly bills, no service contracts

## When Internet Wins

The internet is better when you need:

- **Speed** — file transfers, video, web browsing
- **Global reach** — communicating with people anywhere in the world
- **Always-on services** — email, social media, cloud storage
- **Rich media** — photos, voice calls, video chat

## They're Complementary

Mesh networking isn't a replacement for the internet — it's a complement. Most Meshtastic users have internet access most of the time. The mesh serves as a backup, a local communication tool, and a way to stay connected when the internet isn't available.

Think of it like having a torch. You don't need it when the lights are on. But when the power goes out, you're very glad you have one.
